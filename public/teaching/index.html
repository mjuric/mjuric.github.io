ASTR 597: Big Data in Astronomy: Introduction to Large Surveys

Location When: 3:30-5:00, Mon & Tue, Winter quarter 2014-2015 Where: PAB B356A (Physics-Astronomy Building, 3rd Floor) Description While surveys have been with us since the dawn of science, the precision, depth, and the several orders of magnitude larger datasets of today are unprecedented. Today, major astronomical surveys are routinely collecting hundreds of terabytes of images, creating databases with billions of objects and several billion measurements. This shifts the focus from data collection to data mining, from examination of small samples to studies of entire populations, from identification by eye to automated algorithmic search.
